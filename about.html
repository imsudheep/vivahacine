OUR BEGINNING

Vivaha Cinematics began not in a studio, but in the quiet hours of a Kodaikanal morning, watching light move across still water. That moment—the way the world looked when no one was posing for it—became the foundation of everything we build.

We believe the best images are the ones that happen when you forget the camera exists. Our work is a practice of observation, patience, and deep respect for the unscripted. Every wedding we document is a world unto itself—and we approach each one as if it's the first story we've ever told.

Over the past decade, that philosophy has carried us across hundreds of celebrations—from intimate elopements in the Western Ghats to grand estate weddings along the Mahabalipuram coast. Through it all, the principle remains unchanged: show up with presence, move with care, and let the story reveal itself.

Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu — 2024
LEAD ARTIST & FOUNDER

RAJIV MEHTA

Photographer · Cinematographer · Storyteller

With over a decade of experience spanning three continents, Rajiv has built a practice defined by restraint and emotional intelligence. His work has been featured in Vogue, Martha Stewart Weddings, and Style Me Pretty—but his greatest pride remains the trust couples place in him on the most important day of their lives.

“I don't direct love. I witness it. My job is to be present enough to see the moments that matter—and quiet enough not to interrupt them.”
THE VIVAHA WAY

PRINCIPLES THAT GUIDE US

01

Light over Setup

We chase natural light, not artificial perfection. The best images come from understanding how light falls, moves, and transforms a space—not from orchestrating it.

02

Quiet Presence

We move through your day like guests, not directors. Our presence should never disrupt the moment—only preserve it. The quieter we are, the more honest your story becomes.

03

Legacy over Trends

We don't chase trends. Every frame is composed with the knowledge that these images will be looked at for generations. Timelessness is not accidental—it's intentional.
